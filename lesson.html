Area of a Parallelogram

Without using the formula listed in Theorem 100, you can find the area of a
given parallelogram using the "cut-and-paste" method.

The area of a parallelogram is the same as a rectangle with the same height/altitude and base/width!

Theorem 100: A = bh

The area of a parallelogram can be found using the formula A = bh.

A refers to the area, b to the length of the base, and h to the height of the parallelogram.
(Area = base multipled by height)

Area of a Triangle

The area of all triangles can be shown as ½ of the area of any parallelogram that has the same base and height.

The area of a parallelogram is the length of its base to its height.

Each parallelogram as 2 identical triangles put together. Since these 2 triangles are identical, they will
have the same area, so each triangle will have half the area of the parallelogram.

Theorem 101: A = ½bh

The area of a triangle can be found by the formula A= ½ bh.

A refers to the area, b to the length of the base, and h to the height of the triangle.
(Area = base multiplied by height, then divided by 2)
